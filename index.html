Sir James Dyson

Sir James Dyson is a British inventor, industrial designer, landowner and entrepreneur who founded Dyson Ltd. He is best known as the inventor of the Dual Cyclone bagless vacuum cleaner, which works on the principle of cyclonic separation.

Here's a timeline of how Sir James Dyson started to be Against the Odds:

  • 1979 - Got the idea of creating vacuum cleaner that would not lose suction power as it picked up dirt with cyclonic separation
  • 1980 - Filed series of patents for dual cyclone vacuum cleaner EP0037674
  • 1983 - Invented the world's first cyclonic bagless vacuum cleaner G-Force
  • 1986 - Launched G-Force in Japan as no manufacturers in UK agreed to handle this product
  • 1991 - G-Force won the International Design Fair Prize
  • 1993 - Founded Dyson Limited
  • 1992 - Breakthrough in UK market with the slogan "Say Goodbye to the Bag"
  • 2001 - Released Dual Cyclone 7 (DC07). It is the first vacuum to use "cyclone separation" (Root Cyclone Technology)
  • 2005 - Dyson cleaners had become the market leaders in the United States by value (though not by number of units sold)
  • 2006 - Launched Dyson Airblade (hand dryer)
  • 2009 - Launched Air Multiplier (bladeless fan)
